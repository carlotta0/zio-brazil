Brigadeiros

Famous in Brazil, brigadeiro are small sweet balls made from chocolate. It only takes three ingredients to make these soft and delicious balls that melt in your mouth. Like our truffles and other sweets made as a reuse of festive sweets, they are perfect to serve at the end of a meal or as a sweet treat in the afternoon. Covered with chocolate sprinkles, they are crunchy on the outside and soft and creamy on the inside! Brigadeiro are really simple to prepare, once tried we are sure that you will use them to enrich your birthday buffets!

quindim

Quindim s a popular Brazilian baked dessert, made chiefly from sugar, egg yolks and ground coconut. It is a custard and usually presented as an upturned cup with a glistening surface and intensely yellow color.

bolo maracuja

The bolo de maracuja is a Brazilian recipe prepared with maracuja, which we commonly call passion fruit. It is a bole, or a cake, usually in the shape of a donut, with the juice of the passion fruit in the mixture and then completed with a syrup always based on the same fruit. The result is a very simple yet refined dessert, a greedy way to bring an explosion of tropical taste to the table;)

bolo nega maluca

The origin of this dessert dates back to the times of slavery in Brazil but the creator of the dessert is an African. Legend has it that one day Nega Maluca, while carelessly preparing the cake for his mistress, by mistake he put more chocolate than he should and, frightened, he mixed everything in the same oven.
